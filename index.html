My Projects And Experiences





Visualization of Data on Smartwatch Interfaces
May 2022 - Nov 2024

About

As part of my Master’s program, I conducted an experimental investigation into the effects of visualization on understanding data displayed on smartwatches for runners.A portion of my thesis was published in the prestigious 2024 IEEE Visualization and Visual Analytics (VIS)conference.


Objectives

  • Researched user preferences for smartwatch face designs during running activities, focusing on usability and comprehension.
  • Designed and implemented various visualizations for key data fields such as speed, distance, and heart rate.
  • Analyzed and compared user feedback and performance to identify preferred visualization styles.


  • My Role :
    • Researcher
    • Designer
  • Methods :
    • Design and Prototyping
    • Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) Evaluation
    • User-Centered Design Principles
    • Realistic Testing Environment
    • Data Collection, Quantitative and Qualitative Analysis



Data Visualization Design at SON Company
Jan 2015 - Oct 2019

About

I started Data Visualization design as an internship in SON company when I was still a second-year student at university in order to gain work experience and satisfy requirements for qualification. In this project, we created an interactive data visualization for Pasargad Oil Company, the largest producer and exporter of bitumen in Iran. The dashboard`s aim was to define and analyze performance indicators in the form of reports and managerial dashboards in various functional areas (commerce, finance, human resources, production and operations, strategy, etc).





Objectives

  • Provide reports for users of different groups based on existing indicators.
  • Add new indicators and create corresponding report.
  • Mechanize the receiving of required information for reports, which is currently executed in manual procedures.
  • Ability to add other data sources to the system by the system administrator.

Teaching Experience
Sep 2022 - Dec 2024

About

For over two years, I have been teaching the Human-Computer Interaction (HCI) course, focusing on the design and use of computer technology, particularly the interfaces between people and computers. In this course, I guide students in observing how humans interact with computers and encourage them to design technologies that enhance efficiency, visibility, learnability, and usability. Together, we explore the principles of user-centered design and apply them to create innovative solutions. As part of the course, students design a product by applying the concepts learned. To inspire and guide them, I showcase apps and interfaces I’ve designed as practical examples.
